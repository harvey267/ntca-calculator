## Section 1: Program Overview
The NTCA R&S Program is a Cooperative and Small Employer Charity (CSEC) defined benefit pension plan under IRS rules, sponsored by NTCA–The Rural Broadband Association. It's funded by employer contributions (no employee contributions required) and provides lifetime annuities or lump sums based on your service and compensation.  

- **Key Features** (from 2025 Summary Plan Description [SPD] and Specifications):
  - Normal Retirement Age (NRA): 65.
  - Vesting: 5 years of participation.
  - Benefit Formula: 1.9% (your employer's elected rate) × High-5 Compensation × Years of Participation.
  - Funding Status: 86.51% funded as of 12/31/2024 (from 2024 Annual Funding Notice), using 7.50% interest rate for liabilities.
  - PBGC Guarantee: Up to ~$7,431/month at age 65 (2025 max; adjusted annually).
- **CSEC-Specific Rules**: As a CSEC plan, funding uses a fixed 7.50% rate (not segment rates), but lump sums follow standard §417(e) rules (segment rates and mortality).

**Math Insight**: The program's actuarial value of liabilities was $2,369,764,258 in 2024, calculated as PV = ∑ [Expected Benefits_t / (1 + 0.075)^t], where t is years from valuation date.

## Section 2: Eligibility and Participation
- **Eligibility**: Employees of participating NTCA members (e.g., your company) become participants after meeting age/service requirements (typically age 21 and 1 year of service, per SPD Section I).
- **Participation**: Credited for periods with 1,000+ hours/year (if elected). Partial years count as fractions (e.g., 2 months = 0.167 years).

## Section 3: Benefit Accrual Formula
Your accrued benefit is calculated annually and frozen upon termination (unless you return).  

- **Formula** (SPD Section II):  
  Annual Normal Retirement Benefit (NRB) = Benefit Rate × High-5 Compensation × Years of Participation.  
  - Benefit Rate: 1.9% (0.019), elected by your employer (from Adoption Agreement in Specifications).  
  - High-5 Compensation: Average of your 5 highest compensation years in the last 10 plan years (capped at IRS limit, e.g., $345,000 in 2025). Compensation includes base pay, bonuses, etc.  
    **Math Example**: For $60.74/hour (40 hours/week, 52 weeks = $126,339.20/year), if consistent for 5 years: High-5 = $126,339.20.  
    With 26.167 years: NRB = 0.019 × $126,339.20 × 26.167 = ~$62,843/year ($5,237/month unreduced).  
  - Years of Participation: Full years + fractions (e.g., 26 + 2/12 = 26.167).  

- **Accruals Conversion**: Internally, the rate is converted to "accruals" by dividing by an actuarial factor (e.g., 0.1531 for certain periods): Accrual Rate = 1.9% / 0.1531 ≈ 0.1241. Total benefit = Sum of annual accruals × High-5 (from DOCUMENT Section 1).

**Expert Math**: If compensation varies, High-5 = (Sum of Top 5 Years) / 5. For projections, assume salary growth (e.g., 3% annual): Future High-5 = Current × (1.03)^n.

## Section 4: High-5 Compensation Details
- **Definition**: Average annual compensation over your 5 highest-paid years in the last 10 plan years before termination/retirement (SPD Section II).
- **Inclusions/Exclusions**: Base salary, bonuses, commissions; excludes overtime (as in your case), reimbursements, etc. Capped at IRS §401(a)(17) limit ($345,000 in 2025).
- **Calculation Example**: $60.74/hour × 40 hours/week × 52 weeks = $126,339.20/year. If no raises, High-5 = $126,339.20. With 2% annual raise: Year 1 $126,339; Year 2 $128,866; etc.—average top 5.

**Expert Tip**: If terminated early, High-5 is frozen at termination date (SPD Section III-2). Track via Pension Point portal.

## Section 5: Rule of 85 and Retirement Timing
- **Rule of 85**: Unreduced benefits if Age + Years of Participation ≥ 85 and age ≥ 55 (from DOCUMENT and NTCA specs). No early reduction penalty.
  - **Math**: Age (whole years) + Service (whole + fractions) = 85+. Example: Age 55 + 30 years = 85—unreduced annuity starts at 55.
  - Your Calculator: At May 31, 2029 (age 55), unreduced $5,226.71/month, implying you hit 85 then (projected service ~30 years).
- **Early Retirement**: Age 55+ with 10+ years—reduced 0.25%/month before 65 + 0.25%/month before 60 (up to 60% max at 55 without Rule of 85).
  - Formula: Reduced NRB = Unreduced × (1 - Reduction %).
- **Late Retirement**: Increase 0.25%/month after 65 + ongoing accruals (SPD Section V).
- **Deferred Vested**: If vested but terminate before 55, benefits at 65 or reduced early.

**Expert Math**: Reduction % = 0.0025 × Months Early (to 65) + 0.0025 × Months Early (to 60 if applicable). For Rule of 85, % = 0.

## Section 6: Annuity Options and Payments
- **Standard Form**: 10-Year Certain and Life Annuity—monthly payments for life, guaranteed 120 months (SPD Section VI).
- **Options** (actuarially equivalent using 6% interest + 2009 mortality table for post-2011 service, per DOCUMENT Section 3):
  - Joint & Survivor (QJSA for married): Reduced monthly (e.g., 50% survivor = ~10-15% reduction) to provide spouse benefit.
  - Leveling: Higher monthly until age 62/SS age, then drop to level with SS.
  - Guaranteed: Fixed payments for set period or amount.
- **Interest Rate Impact**: Fixed 6% for equivalence (not segment rates). Higher rates would mean less reduction for survivors, but it's locked.

**Math Example**: For $5,237/month life only, 50% J&S reduction factor ~0.90 (depending on ages): Reduced = $5,237 × 0.90 = $4,713.

## Section 7: Lump Sum Calculations – Detailed Methodology
Lump sums are the present value (PV) of your annuity stream, using IRS minimum present value segment rates (spot rates, no 24-month average) and mortality tables under §417(e) (from Rev. Rul. 2007-67 and PPA §302). Full or partial lump sums available if PV > $1,000 (Specifications Page 48, truncated in attachments).

- **Segment Rates Overview** (from IRS Minimum Present Value page): Derived from corporate bond yields (AAA-BBB rated). Three segments discount based on payout timing:
  - Segment 1: Benefits in 0-5 years (months 1-60).
  - Segment 2: 5-20 years (months 61-240).
  - Segment 3: >20 years (months 241+).
  - Latest (August 2025): 4.20% / 5.29% / 6.08%. Lower rates = higher lump sum (inverse).
- **Mortality Tables**: Unisex static tables (e.g., 2026 from Notice 2025-40: RP-2014 base projected to 2026 with MP-2021 scale). Provides survival probability p_t at each month t.

- **Full Math Formula**:  
  Lump Sum PV = ∑_{t=1}^{T} [Monthly Annuity × p_t × (1 / (1 + r_segment / 12)^t)]  
  Where:
  - t = month from ASD (T ~360-480 months to age 100-120).
  - p_t = Survival probability from mortality table (1 at t=0, decreases over time).
  - r_segment = Segment 1 if t ≤ 60; Segment 2 if 61 ≤ t ≤ 240; Segment 3 if t > 240.
  - For reduced annuity (if no Rule of 85): Multiply by reduction factor.

- **Step-by-Step Calculation Example** (using your calculator's $5,227/month at age 55, 2029 ASD; assume 2025 rates for illustration):
  1. Project annuity stream: $5,227/month for ~360 months (to age 85, adjusted by mortality).
  2. Discount each month: For t=60 (5 years): DF = 1 / (1 + 0.042/12)^60 ≈ 0.810 (using 4.20%).
  3. Apply mortality: e.g., p_60 ≈ 0.98 at age 60.
  4. Sum: PV ≈ $900,000 (matches calculator; code execution verified similar approx PV ~$962k without mortality, reduced to ~$900k with).
- **Partial Lump Sum**: Elected % of PV as cash; remaining annuity reduced proportionally (using 417(e) rates for PV). Formula: Reduced Annuity = Full Annuity × (1 - Lump % / PV Factor).

**Expert Tip**: NTCA uses August prior-year lookback for calendar stability (inferred from calculator patterns—jumps in Jan). Code to model: Use Python with sympy for exact sums or pandas for tables.

## Section 8: Partial Lump Sum and Interest Rate Impacts
- **Partial Option**: Up to 50% of PV as lump sum, remaining as reduced annuity (DOCUMENT Section 2B). Higher rates lower lump PV, potentially increasing remaining annuity.
- **Interest Impacts** (DOCUMENT Section 1-4): Funding at 7.5%, but lump sums use segments. Annuities fixed once started; rates affect conversions (6% fixed for options).
- **Math**: For partial, Lump = Elected % × Full PV; Reduced NRB = (1 - Elected %) × NRB.
